Understanding the Five-Step Process for Better Mental Health

1

Identify the Situation

Begin by clearly identifying the specific situation or event that triggered your emotional response. What exactly happened? When and where did it occur? Be as specific as possible about the circumstances that led to your current emotional state.

2

Identify the Feelings

Recognize and name the emotions you're experiencing. Are you feeling anxious, angry, sad, frustrated, or something else? Rate the intensity of these feelings on a scale of 1-10. Understanding your emotional response is crucial for the CBT process.

3

Identify the Thoughts

Examine the thoughts that are running through your mind about the situation. What are you telling yourself? What assumptions are you making? These automatic thoughts often occur quickly and may not be immediately obvious, so take time to reflect on them.

4

Identify the Thought Distortion

Look for cognitive distortions in your thinking patterns. Common distortions include all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing, overgeneralization, and personalization. Recognizing these patterns helps you understand how your thoughts might be contributing to your emotional distress.

5

Come Up with Revised Thinking/Next Action

Develop more balanced and realistic thoughts about the situation. Consider alternative perspectives and evidence that might challenge your initial thoughts. Then, decide on constructive actions you can take to address the situation or improve your emotional well-being.
